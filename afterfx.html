Rotoscoping

Rotoscoping is a powerful technique in After Effects that allows you to isolate objects or subjects from footage, frame by frame, creating a silhouette (mask) that can be filled, cut out, or manipulated independently from the background. It's especially useful for visual effects and compositing, enabling editors to create stunning scenes by combining elements from different sources.

3D Tracking and more

3D tracking in After Effects allows you to analyze footage and extract information about the movement of real-world objects. This technique lets you seamlessly integrate new elements into your footage with correct position, scale, orientation, and motion relative to the physical environment in the video. It's a key skill for adding visual effects that blend naturally with your original scenes.

Advanced Compositing

Advanced Compositing in After Effects takes your skills to the top tier, enabling you to blend multiple layers of video and graphics seamlessly. This expert tutorial guides you through the sophisticated techniques of combining visual elements from various sources into a single, cohesive scene. Enhance your ability to create complex visual narratives that captivate your audience.

The king of words: Scripting

Scripting in After Effects unlocks a new realm of possibilities, allowing you to automate repetitive tasks, generate complex animations, and manipulate properties with precision. This expert-level tutorial delves into the power of scripting to extend the functionality of After Effects, teaching you how to write and apply scripts that can dramatically enhance your workflow and creative output.
